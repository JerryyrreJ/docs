Sub's subscription list is your central view of every recurring charge. From here you can add new services, update existing ones, and stay on top of upcoming renewals — all stored locally in your browser without needing an account.

## Adding a subscription

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Add subscription">
    Open the main subscription list and click the **Add subscription** button in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter name, category, amount, and currency">
    Type the service name (for example, Netflix), choose a category, enter the cost per billing period, and select the currency from the supported list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a billing period">
    Select **monthly**, **yearly**, or **custom**. Choosing custom lets you define a specific date interval so Sub can calculate renewals for non-standard billing cycles.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the last payment date">
    Set the date of the most recent charge. Sub uses this date together with the billing period to automatically calculate the **next renewal date** — you never have to set it manually.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle notifications">
    Enable or disable renewal reminders for this specific subscription using the notification toggle. The setting is per-subscription, so you can silence reminders for subscriptions you don't want to track closely.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The subscription appears immediately in your list, and the next renewal date is calculated and displayed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Subscription fields

Every subscription record stores the following information:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Name | The service name (e.g. Netflix, Spotify) |
| Category | A group used for analytics — choose from built-in or custom categories |
| Amount | The cost charged each billing period |
| Currency | One of the ten supported currencies: CNY, USD, EUR, JPY, GBP, AUD, CAD, CHF, HKD, or SGD |
| Period | The billing frequency: `monthly`, `yearly`, or `custom` |
| Last payment date | The date of the most recent charge |
| Next renewal | Calculated automatically from the last payment date plus the billing period |
| Custom date | An interval in days used to calculate renewals when the period is set to `custom` |
| Notification | Per-subscription toggle to enable or disable renewal reminders |

## Editing a subscription

To update any field, click the **edit icon** on the subscription card. Change whichever fields you need and click **Save**. Sub recalculates the next renewal date if you update the last payment date or billing period.

## Deleting a subscription

Click the **delete** option on the subscription card. Sub removes the record immediately from local storage. This action cannot be undone, so export a backup first if you want to keep a copy.

## Sorting

Use the sort control to order your subscriptions by any of these fields:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Name" icon="a-z">
    Alphabetical order by service name.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Amount" icon="dollar-sign">
    Lowest to highest, or highest to lowest cost.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Next renewal" icon="calendar">
    Soonest or furthest upcoming renewal date.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Category" icon="tag">
    Grouped alphabetically by category name.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Date added" icon="clock">
    Most recently added or oldest first.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Each sort field supports both **ascending** and **descending** order. Click the sort toggle to flip the direction.

## Filtering and searching

- **Filter by category** — select a category from the filter menu to show only subscriptions in that group.
- **Search by name** — type in the search box to instantly narrow the list to matching service names.

Combine both controls to find exactly the subscription you're looking for.

## Monthly vs. yearly view

Toggle between **Monthly** and **Yearly** view at the top of the dashboard. Sub converts all subscription amounts to your selected base currency and displays the total cost for the chosen period. This gives you a clear picture of your short-term and long-term spending at a glance.

<Tip>
  Switch to Yearly view before your renewal season to see the full annual cost of all your subscriptions in one number.
</Tip>
