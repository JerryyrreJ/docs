Categories let you group subscriptions — for example, Streaming, Productivity, or Gaming — and power the category breakdown chart in Analytics. Every subscription belongs to exactly one category, so assigning them thoughtfully gives you more meaningful spending reports.

## Built-in categories

Sub ships with the following categories ready to use:

- Entertainment
- Software
- Music
- Productivity
- Other

These built-in categories cover the most common subscription types. They are available immediately when you add your first subscription — no configuration needed.

<Note>
  Built-in categories cannot be permanently deleted. If you don't use a built-in category, you can hide it from the category manager so it no longer appears in dropdowns and charts. Hidden built-in categories can be restored at any time.
</Note>

## Creating a custom category

If the built-in categories don't match your needs, create your own in seconds.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the category manager">
    Navigate to **Settings** and select **Categories** to open the category manager.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add category">
    Click the **Add category** button at the bottom of the category list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a name">
    Type a name for your category. Names can contain letters, numbers, spaces, hyphens, and `&`. The maximum length is 50 characters, and names must be unique.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The new category is immediately available in the category dropdown when you add or edit any subscription.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Editing and deleting custom categories

- **Rename** — click the edit icon next to any custom category, type the new name, and save. Existing subscriptions assigned to that category update automatically.
- **Delete** — click the delete icon next to a custom category to remove it permanently. Subscriptions previously assigned to a deleted category fall back to the **Uncategorized** fallback group. The *Uncategorized* category cannot itself be deleted.

<Tip>
  Drag categories in the category manager to reorder them. The order you set here controls the order they appear in dropdowns and in the Analytics category breakdown chart.
</Tip>

## Assigning a category to a subscription

When you add or edit a subscription, choose a category from the **Category** dropdown. The dropdown shows all visible built-in and custom categories in the order you have arranged them.

## Categories in Analytics

Each category you use appears as a separate slice in the category breakdown chart on the Analytics page. Subscriptions without a specific category appear under **Other** or **Uncategorized**. The more precisely you categorise your subscriptions, the more informative the breakdown becomes.
